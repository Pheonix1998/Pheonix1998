### 1. 🏃‍♂️ HealthTech User Engagement & Retention Analysis

<a href="https://github.com/TATHAGATA23071998/PROJECTS/tree/main/STRAVA%20PROJECT%20FILES">
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Project_Repository-181717?style=for-the-badge&logo=github&logoColor=white">
</a>

**The Business Problem:** The company was experiencing severe mid-week user drop-offs and low overall engagement, threatening Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV). Leadership needed a data-driven strategy to pivot the product from a passive "tracker" to an active "health partner."

**Core Metrics Tracked:** Average Daily Steps (~7,765) | Active Minutes (~231) | Average Sleep Time (~5.8 hrs) | Daily Active Users

**Executive Insights:**
* **The "Nurses Day" Anomaly:** Discovered a massive retention surge on May 12th (*International Nurses Day*), revealing healthcare workers as the core "Power User" segment.
* **Biological Churn:** Correlated mid-week app abandonment directly to chronic sleep deprivation, with users averaging a dangerously low 5.8 hours of sleep.
* **Constrained Rhythms:** Identified strict dual-peak engagement windows (12:00–14:00 and 17:00–19:00), confirming usage is highly dictated by professional shift schedules.

**Strategic Recommendations:**
* **Product Strategy:** Deploy a "Recovery Module" demonstrating how 30 extra minutes of sleep improves next-day productivity to protect mid-week retention.
* **Marketing Strategy:** Confine push notifications exclusively to the identified lunch and post-shift windows, and launch targeted B2B campaigns aimed at healthcare workers.
* **Growth Strategy:** Develop a "VIP" tier utilizing the behaviors of Power Users to pull the "Casual" majority into higher engagement brackets.

<i><b>Tools:</b> Python | SQL (SQL Server) | Tableau | ETL Pipeline Design</i><br>

### 2. 🚖 Ride-Hailing Operations: Revenue Leakage & Supply-Side Bottlenecks  

<a href="https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/tathagata.chakraborty5102/viz/RIDESERVICEANALYSISDASHBOARD/Dashboard">
<img src="https://img.shields.io/badge/Project_Repository-181717?style=for-the-badge&logo=github&logoColor=white">
</a>

**The Business Problem:** Regional operations suffered a massive 38% ride failure rate. The executive team needed to definitively answer whether this was a demand-side marketing issue or a supply-side operational bottleneck to stop ₹56.5M in revenue leakage.

**Core Metrics Tracked:** Total Booking Value (₹56.53M) | Successful Booking Rate (62.09%) | Total Ride Volume (103,024) | Wait Times (TAT)

**Executive Insights:**
* **Supply-Side Failure:** The interactive dashboard conclusively proved the bottleneck was 100% supply-side, driven primarily by driver cancellations, ruling out customer demand issues.
* **Operational "Gaming":** Uncovered widespread driver gaming behavior—accepting rides but remaining stationary to force the customer to initiate the cancellation to avoid platform penalties.
* **Payment Risk Profile:** Correlated payment methods with cancellation risk, revealing that while "Cash" drives volume, it carries the highest operational failure rate.

**Strategic Recommendations:**
* **Operational Policy:** Implement a GPS based penalty system to automatically reassign stationary drivers, eliminating customer-side friction.
* **Payment Shift:** Launch aggressive discount campaigns for UPI/Digital payments to lock in platform revenue and reduce the cash-based cancellation risk.
* **Partnerships:** Partner with local insurance and maintenance networks to keep vehicles on the road, reducing mechanical-related driver cancellations.

<details><summary><b>🛠️ Technical Execution (Click to expand)</b></summary>
<i><b>Tools Used:</b> SQL Server (SSMS) | Python (Pandas) | Tableau | Streamlit</i><br>
Engineered a highly optimized Star Schema in SQL Server to process 100,000+ transaction records, ensuring instant load times for executive BI dashboards. Architected a "Junk Dimension" to handle messy, unstructured cancellation text, drastically reducing computational load. Imputed missing wait-time values using statistical medians to preserve ride volume integrity.
</details>
